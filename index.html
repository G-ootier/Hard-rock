WHY DO ROCKS TENSE UP?

Rocks were formed during the most violent period in Earth's history volcanic explosions, asteroid impacts, tectonic grinding.

Every rock alive today is a survivor of unimaginable geological violence. The tensing reflex is not a defense mechanism. It is a trauma response.

Rocks don't tense up because they are strong. They tense up because they are, deep down, absolutely traumatised.

Sand is rock dandruff.
